About the Income Approach: The income approach values a property based on its capacity to generate rental income. The gross yield compares annual rent to the purchase price. Most Melbourne residential properties yield between 2.5–4.5%. If the implied value from income is significantly lower than the direct comparison estimate, the property may be priced for owner-occupiers rather than investors.

About Land & Improvements: This approach splits the total value into land value and improvements (buildings). In high-demand suburbs, land often comprises 60–75% of total value. A high land-to-total ratio suggests strong development potential and land banking value.

Methodology — How this valuation was produced
01

Comparable Sales

Recent sales of similar properties are gathered and weighted by recency. Sales within 60 days carry full weight; older sales are time-adjusted using the market trend factor.

02

Adjustment Factors

Each valuation factor (condition, location, market trend, zoning) is applied as a multiplier to the weighted comp average. Bedroom and bathroom count are adjusted at 4% and 2.5% per unit respectively.

03

Cross-Check Approaches

The direct comparison result is checked against the income approach (capitalised rental value) and land value breakdown to identify consistency or flag anomalies.

04

Confidence Rating

A confidence rating is assigned based on the spread between comparable sales. A spread under 10% is high confidence; 10–20% moderate; over 20% is low confidence and requires further investigation.

Disclaimer: This tool provides an indicative market value estimate only and does not constitute a formal valuation for mortgage, legal, or statutory purposes. Estimates are based on inputs provided and simplified adjustment factors. For a certified valuation, engage a registered valuer under the Valuers Registration Act.
